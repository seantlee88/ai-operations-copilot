Section 1 – Introduction
1.1 Purpose
This handbook serves to familiarize employees with the policies, procedures, and key practices of Microbial Remediation Solutions (“the Company”). The guidelines set forth herein supersede any prior policies or rules, whether communicated verbally or in writing. All employees are expected to adhere to the standards and requirements detailed in this handbook. The Company reserves the right to interpret and apply the provisions of this handbook at its sole discretion and may make exceptions to policy as appropriate.

1.2 Changes of Policy
Microbial Remediation Solutions reserves the right to modify the contents of this handbook at any time, and at its sole discretion. No changes to these policies are valid unless made official by the Company. You will be notified in writing of any updates to the employee handbook and are responsible for understanding and adhering to all current policies. If you have any questions regarding the information in this handbook, please contact the Operations Manager.

Section 2 – Terms & Definitions of Employment
2.1 At-Will Employment
Employment at Microbial Remediation Solutions is considered “at-will.” This means that either the employee or the Company may terminate the employment relationship at any time, with or without notice, and for any lawful reason. The Company also reserves the right to change or modify an employee’s compensation, benefits, or work assignments at its sole discretion.
2.2 Job Classification
Exempt vs. Non-Exempt
Non-exempt employees are entitled to minimum wage and overtime pay. Exempt employees are not eligible for overtime pay and meet legal exemption requirements.
Regular vs. Temporary
Full-time: 32+ hours per week
Part-time: Less than 32 hours per week
Temporary: Employed for a limited time (not to exceed six months)
Independent Contractors
Independent contractors are not employees and are not eligible for company benefits.

Section 3 – Payroll
3.1 Payment Schedule
Employees are paid twice per month:
1st–15th → Paid on last day of month
16th–End of month → Paid on the 15th
If payday falls on a weekend or holiday, pay will be issued the previous business day.
3.2 Wages
Wages are based on experience and performance.
Overtime
Non-exempt employees receive 1.5x pay for hours worked over 40 in a workweek. Overtime must be approved in advance.
3.3 Withholdings
Deductions include:
Social Security
Federal Income Tax
State Income Tax
Medicare
WA Paid Family Medical Leave
WA Cares
WA Labor & Industries

Section 6 – Rules of Conduct
6.1 On the Job
Reporting for Work
Employees must report to work on time. Two consecutive no-call/no-show days will be considered voluntary resignation.
End of Shift
Your shift ends when assigned work is completed.
Notification Procedure
Employees must call (not text) at least 30 minutes before shift if late or absent.
Planned Absences
Submit requests at least 2 weeks in advance.
Sick Leave
Doctor’s note required after 3 consecutive missed days.
Tardiness Discipline
Verbal Warning
Written Warning
Suspension
Termination

Clocking In
Non-exempt employees must clock in at the start of shift and clock out at the end. Timecard changes require supervisor approval.

Meals & Breaks
10-minute paid break per 4 hours worked
30-minute unpaid meal break for shifts over 5 hours
Must clock in/out for meal breaks

Dress Code
Work boots (steel toe not required) and company uniforms required.

Uniforms
Uniforms must be returned upon termination. Missing uniforms will be deducted from final paycheck.

Personal Cell Phone Policy
Employees must provide their own smartphone capable of photos, scanning, and documentation for work.

6.2 Rules & Policies
Confidentiality
Employees may not share company or personal information without authorization.
Discrimination & Harassment
Discrimination or harassment will result in disciplinary action up to termination.
Drug & Alcohol Policy
The following are prohibited:
Alcohol or illegal drugs at work
Misuse of prescription drugs
Being under the influence at work
Drug testing may be required if impairment is suspected. Refusal or failed test = termination.



6.3 Disciplinary Action
Reasons may include:
Excessive lateness
Poor conduct
Poor communication
Unauthorized use of company property
Disclosure of company information
Drugs, alcohol, weapons
Harassment or discrimination
Policy violations
Discipline may include warning, suspension, demotion, or termination.

6.4 Workplace Inspections
The Company may inspect:
Offices
Computers
Vehicles
Personal belongings on company property
Security camera footage
Refusal to comply may result in discipline.

Section 7 – Accident Prevention Program (APP)

Objective
To maintain a safe work environment and prevent accidents and injuries in compliance with OSHA and EPA regulations.

Responsibilities
Management
Enforce safety rules
Conduct inspections
Provide PPE
Provide safety training
Investigate accidents
Employees
Work safely
Report hazards immediately
Use PPE
Attend safety meetings
Only operate equipment if authorized
Training
Employees will be trained before performing job tasks and on PPE usage.
Hazard Identification
Includes chemicals, dust, electrical hazards, falling objects, etc.
PPE Provided
First Aid Kits
Hard Hats
Safety Glasses
Hearing Protection
Respirators
Tyvek Suits
Rubber Gloves
Work Gloves
Injury Reporting
All injuries must be reported immediately. OSHA logs are maintained for 5 years.

General Safety Rules
No firearms
No smoking near flammable materials
Only authorized employees operate equipment
